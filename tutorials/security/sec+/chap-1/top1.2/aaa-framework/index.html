AAA for Systems

  1. How would you verify if certain system(device) is allowed on your network.

  2. A system certainly can not type password to verify itself.

  3. So, how can you truely AUTHENTICATE a device ?

  4. You will put a digitally signed certificate on the device.

  5. And other business process can rely on the certificate for access, creating rules, etc.

Authentication

  • Most organizations will have their own Certificate Authority (CA), which will even has its own Certificate signed by Root CA.

  • The organization will create a certificate for the device and digitally signs the certificate with the organization's CA.

  • Now, basically, on a controlled environment, organization itself is responsible for including (putting) that cert in the device.

  • Whenever, the device will want to on the network or access something on the network, this cert can be used as a factor of Authorization.

  • Actually, the CA digital signature is used to validate the certificate.

Authorization

  • Now the system has been authenticated, you should provide access to certain data, apps and resources.

  • A way of doing it is to map User -> Data, but imagine 1000s of users.

  • In such situation, you will often create An Abstraction to Authorization Model, so the mapping will look like Users -> Group(s) -> Data, Apps, Services , this made scaling pretty esay.

Accounting

  • You will need to keep log of :

    1. Login
    2. Activities (Normal - Critical)
    3. Accessed Resources
    4. Logout

  • Log in such a formatted way (date, time, detail, level, etc.), so that it will help analysis team in case of study.
