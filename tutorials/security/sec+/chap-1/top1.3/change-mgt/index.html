  • It should be well documented.

Change Control

Change control should be done in controlled environment because a minor change in software, patch in app or even single firewall/switch change in configuration could impact large porton of your organization.

  • Complete request forms

  • Determine the purpose of change

  • Identify the scope of change

  • Schedule Date/Time of change

  • Determine affected systems and the impact

  • Analyze Risk

  • Get Approval from change control board

  • Get end-user acceptance after the change

Owners are not always the impact scope, you should consider all the stakeholders.

Not everyone should be allowed to make the change.

Impact Analysis

  • Determine the Risk Value i.e. What if it does not work ?

    • Fix may break something else
    • OS may fail to adapt change
    • Data or file may corrupt

  • What time of the year change be made ? Definitely not when involved largely on production.

  • Risk of not making the change ?

    • Can you tollerate Security Vulnerability ?
    • Will you be OK when app won't be available if you don't update ?
    • Can you manage downtime i.e the effect of not making change ?

Test Results

  • Create a Sandbox Environment.

  • You are free to make mistakes on this alike (not connected to production) environment.

  • Test and confirm before deployment.

  • Always have backout plan i.e. related to How to go back if something is messed up ?

    • Maybe you will save old config file
    • Maybe you backup all your data
    • Maybe you upload somewhere in the cloud
