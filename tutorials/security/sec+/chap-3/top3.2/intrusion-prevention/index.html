Intrusion Prevention System (IPS)

  • It is designed to monitor the traffic that traverse the network at the real time.

  • If it sees any unwanted packet, pre definied signatures or sign of malware or or known vulnerabilities or something dangerous it will stop that from being executed even if firewall has allowed them for some reasons.

  • It may work with IDS that will alert IPS and it will block them.

Failure Modes

  • We may configure many devices for security, but they can fail.

  • If fails occurs may be due to crash, hardware failure or etc. There can be :

    • Open Fail
    • Close Fail

  • This simple means either to pass on traffic when device fails (open fail) or not (close fail).

  • This depends on the architecture of your network and how secure you want the environment be.

Passive Monitoring

  • Real time traffic block may be the reason of downtime because it may be aggressive and blocking legitimate traffic also for some reasons.

  • In this case, you may want to have different architecture. Insted of placing IPS in-line of the communication, you will like to put it behind the communication and send the copy of every packets received to monitor.

  • There should be some mechanism like in switch we have port mirror, if it is not available we can set a network tap. This will help in duplicating and sending traffic to IPS for evaluation.
