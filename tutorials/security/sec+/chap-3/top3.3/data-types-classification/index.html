Data Types

Data is import assset for an organization. Data loss in significant amount, may even shut down the company.

Regulated Data

  • Managed by third party, because you are asking them to keep, they will decide how to secure your data and they will be responsible for security.

  • Decided by your clients or parters on how to keep the data, because they are paying you.

  • Government laws may restrict on how you keep and make data available.

Trade secret

  • Organizations secret formulas of trade, reciepe, deals, etc. be kept private. (often competative org, want to access these data)

  • Fellow competative companies may want your reciepe for chips production as they spend millions of dollar to get the appropriate satisfaction level of comsumers.

  • These trade secret are unique to organization.

intellectual property

  • Made available to others like copyright, etc.

  • But should be protected with copyright law, trademark law and others.

Legal information

  • Legal papers should be made available to public such as court orders, docs, etc.

  • But those which should be kept private should be private.

  • Personally Identifiable Information (PII) must be private.

Financial Information

  • Internal financial info and details.

  • Customer information.

  • Payment records.

  • Credit card data, bank statements, loans, etc.

  • But P/L, balance sheet , expenditure may be made public to shareholders or stakeholders.

Data Redability

Human readable

  • Simple text file data are readable.

  • clear and obvious are human eye readable.

Non-human readable

  • QR codes, Bar codes data can't be identified by humans.

  • Encoded and encrypted data are not understandable.

  • Complex images are difficult to interepret.

Hybrid form

  • Hybrid form of data is that some part of data can be identified by humans but may need extra knowledge on how the data is intrepreted or presented.

  • csv, xml, json, etc. (read by technicans, hard to read/intrepret by regular users).

  • OR may be number below the barcode (barcode by computers, number by humans).

Classifications

  • Different data has different sensitivity, so can be classified on different ways and following restrictions may be applied :
    • additional permissions
    • different process to view
    • specific person only view
    • restricted network access

Proprietary Data

  • Data only used by specifc organization.

  • Unique to that org like: its property documents.

  • Trade secrets of organization.

PII - Personally Identifiable Information

  • Data that will identify specifc person.

  • Name, DOB, etc.

PHI - Protected Health Information

  • Data regarding medical reports, health info of individual comes under this kind of classification.

  • Health stautus, report, payments, etc.

  • Protect them becasuse for eg : a pacemaker if hacked could end person's life.

Sensitiveness of Data

Sensitive

  • Different data has different sensitivity.

  • Access and Permission be restricted on the basis of public/private/sensitivity.

Confidential

  • More sensitive.

  • Only access to high post person and trusted for org.

  • Only viewed with approval of boss.

Public/Unclassified

  • No restrictions on viewing or sharing data.

Private/Classified/Restricted

  • Restricted access to certain persons or zones or departments.

  • You may have write letter or sign Non Disclosure Agreement just to view the data.

Critical

  • Should always be available, accessible.

  • We should create process and procedures to maintain uptime and availability of that type of data.
