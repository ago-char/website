Data at Rest

  • Refers to data that are not moving or stored in SSD, HDD, etc.

  • To ensure security we would often perform whole disk encryption, or encrypt certain database or certain file or file system depending on OS.

  • We can also apply permissions based on users/groups, also limit the physical access and ensure different authentication mechanisms.

Data at Transit

  • Refers to data that is moving through network or internet.

  • Data, to reach its destination may traverse many switches, routers, firewalls, etc.

  • If data is in plain and someone was able to wiretap the conversation, there is no more we could do to protect the data.

  • So, first if something sensitive info to be traverse the network, we should use encrypted protocol or encryption if protocol implicitly does not support.

  • Other forms of protection could be the use of VPNs, also firewalls to restrict or allow.

Data in Use

  • When considered securing data, data in use is often underlooked, still is one of the important aspect of protection.

  • This is because, when applications are using data they are often decrypted and loaded on RAM, CPU registers and Cache, so attackers are continually targeting data in plain.

  • One way of protecting active data is to use identity management tools to spot anomalies in user behavior and ensure that nobody is committing identify theft in order to access company information. Most identity management tools offer protection through features like single sign-on, two-factor authentication, and access management.

Data Sovereignty

  • Data if recides on particular country or group of contries, law applies to data according to these groups or countries.

  • The data may be subject to wiretap or monitoring or you may have to provide what they need, etc. Also law may prohibit moving data out of the certain teritory.

  • For eg: if you are collecting data of users at EUROPE, you should store them inside EUROPE.

GeoLocations

  • Geolacation of data also plays role in protection and access of data.

  • For eg : certain facilities or websites of tv channels are not available for certain countries.

  • They identify location on the basis of 802.11, LTE, mobile providers, IP, GPS, etc.

  • For local use : if data being accessed within company premisis, then allow, else deny.
