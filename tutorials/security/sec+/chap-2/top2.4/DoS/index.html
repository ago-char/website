Denial of Service

  • Attacker overloading the server, so that no one can gain access. For eg:

    • you may have 10 Ip address for dynamic purpose and if all requested by attacker with DHCP attack, he will have it all and other won't be able to access
    • Server may have implemented 'N' user policy and attacker logging in with all 'N' no. of users could prevent other legitimate user from accessing service.

  • DoS can be perform taking advantage of existing vulnerability.

  • This may be planned by your competitor.

  • This may be a distraction as attacker is keeping you on illusion and attacking other system hardly.

  • Removal of Power is a funny example of DoS, as this is not software based.

You may be the cause of DoS

  • Downloading a large file without bandwidth bound i.e. the file will take the entire bandwidth and you will not be able to perform regular activity normally.

  • You may connect 2 switches together and form a loop if you are not using STP.

Distrubuted DoS (DDoS)

  • There will be series or collection of computer to perform DDoS

  • They are often been compromised for this purpose

  • A central device (attacker) will compromise many devices around the world and the infected devices i.e commonly referred as botnets perfoms anything they receive

  • Attacker will send DDoS request to botnets and they will do exactly the same

  • They tend to send exact high amount of information from very few request and data :

    • For eg: replies of DNS, NTP, ICMP are in very large mount as compared to the requested data size
    • They may spoof target ip and request in behalf of target IP and the requested data, instead of own end will be sent to target automatically by the requested server
    • Obviously this type of attack can be more easy if there is vulnerable target
    • If the target is configured not to receive what they never asked for, this won't be success

Solutions

  • Configuration should be updated.

  • Up to date servers be used.

  • Stateful firewalls be used i.e defing the firewall rules according to state for eg: receive ACK only if SYN is sent, etc.
