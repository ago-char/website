Spyware

  • A malware that is watching your activity and may perform his activity accordingly

  • Monitor your browsing habits, coding, general system habits (what you do inside the computer) and sends back to the attacker.

  • Open your camera and feed to attacker, keylogging etc.

  • Always verify the installation, it is the first place the malware could come from.

  • Use Anti-Malware software like malwarebytes

Bloatware

  • Unwanted pre-installed software on a computer or device -- has been around since the dawn of PCs.

  • Hardware manufacturer or OS may include them.

  • Identify unwanted apps (manually) and delete them.

  • But in many cases you arenot given choice to delete, you can use 3rd party tools that are specially designed to uninstall/delete thse bloatware.

Detection

Often, bloatware is identified by performance degradation. For example, devices weighed down by bloatware can take a long time to boot up, have slowed reaction times or storage issues.

Trialware is a common form of bloatware. Such software applications are included on devices for free when they are purchased and work for a specific trial period until a license is purchased. Adware that pops up on websites or on devices' screens is another form of bloatware, as are system utilities and applications pre-installed on devices by manufacturers.

Bloatware can be detected by end users by looking through the installed applications and identifying any applications they did not install. It can also be detected by an enterprise IT team using a mobile device management tool that lists installed applications.
