Threat Vectors/Scopes are potential entry points for the attacker to come in your organization. They are the methods used by the attacker to gain access. A lot of work goes on finding vulnerabilities (can be software/harware or even employee based) into these area and finally exploit then. As an IT person, you should protect exiting vectors and find and protect other potential vectors.

Message Based

  • You may received phising message on your email/sms.

  • They have story to follow having malicious link, malicious attachments.

  • Don't open those link without verifying sources.

  • Perform Attachment Scans before opening attachment.

Image Based

  • A lot difficult to identify compared to Message Based.

  • SVG image format is more than a image, it contains XML which could inject HTML/JS.

File Based

  • Could be hidden in Adobe PDF.

  • Compressed files (ZIP/RAR, etc.)

  • Microsoft Office Macros or Add-in files.

Voice Based

  • Phishing over the phone i.e Vishing

  • Have a story to follow, exact in details and speaking manner

  • Large-scale phone calls (block them)

  • War Dialing (unpublished phone numbers)

  • Call Tampering

USB Based

  • Infect air gapped networks (no direct network connection to the network).

  • May throw USB drive in parking lot, garden, or maybe in your desk.

  • Can be used as Keyboard.

  • Can run script on plugging.

  • Can transfer your sensitive data very quick automatically.

Software Based

  • Infected Executable

  • Known or Unknown vulnerabilities

  • Vulnerability in server could send malicious message in client

Unsupported systems

  • Vulnerabilities on those system which is no longer support by the vendor.

  • Don't run softwares on those system or isolate if you must need to run.

  • Locate every devices in your org for periodic scans.

Unsecure Network

  • Outdated security protocols (WEP, WPA or even WPA2).

  • Open or rouge wireless networks.

  • Unsecure Wired Networks i.e where 802.1X is not enabled.

  • Bluetooth ( can be used for reconnaissance to locate devices )

Open Ports

  • A door to your computer.

  • Vulnerability in app using that port .

  • Misconfiguration on server using that port.

  • (Create port based firewalls and app based firewalls + packet filtering)

Default Credentials

  • Most IOT devices/routers etc. have default creds

  • Change them

Supply Chain

  • Your vendor may manipulate device specially for you (well someone within vendor manufacturing this who is after you).

  • After manufacture, someone in the supply chain could install or modify (software/hardware).

  • Supplier themself could install backdoors.

  • Counterfeit network equipment maybe distributed which includes backdoors, substandard perfomance and avaibility.
